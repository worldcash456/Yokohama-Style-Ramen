Yokohama-Style Ramen

A comforting bowl of ramen with a deep broth, springy noodles, and a simple but flavorful finish.

About this dish

Yokohama-style ramen is known for its rich, savory broth and a balance of hearty toppings with a slightly lighter, more polished finish. It is cozy, satisfying, and a great choice for a weekend dinner at home.

Ingredients

Serves 2 to 3

  • 2 packs fresh ramen noodles
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, sliced
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 500 ml chicken stock
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon miso paste
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 2 soft-boiled eggs
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1 sheet nori, cut into strips
  • 1 small handful spinach

How to make it

Simple steps

  1. Heat the oil in a saucepan and cook the onion and garlic until fragrant and softened.
  2. Pour in the chicken stock, soy sauce, miso paste, and sesame oil. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Cook the ramen noodles separately according to the package instructions, then drain.
  4. Add the spinach to the broth just before serving so it wilts gently.
  5. Divide the noodles into bowls, pour over the hot broth, and top with the eggs, green onions, and nori.
